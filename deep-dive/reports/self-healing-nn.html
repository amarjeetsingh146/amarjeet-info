Self-Healing Neural Network

Concept • Prototype • Systems Thinking

Modern neural networks assume that all neurons behave correctly during inference. This deep dive explores a different idea: What if a network could detect faulty neurons at runtime and heal itself?

Problem Statement

Traditional fault tolerance relies on hardware redundancy. However, in large-scale or edge deployments, hardware redundancy is expensive, power-hungry, and not always feasible.

Neural networks today fail silently. A single corrupted activation can propagate and degrade the entire output.

Core Idea

Introduce a self-monitoring layer that:

  • Detects anomalous neuron activations
  • Flags unstable or dead neurons
  • Dynamically reroutes computation paths
Instead of trusting every neuron, the network learns to doubt itself.

System Design

The prototype is divided into three logical components:

  1. Activation Monitor – tracks statistical deviation
  2. Fault Classifier – determines temporary vs permanent faults
  3. Rerouting Engine – redirects flow through healthy neurons

This design avoids retraining and operates entirely during inference.

Why This Matters

Self-healing architectures are critical for:

  • Autonomous systems
  • Robotics
  • Space & edge AI
  • Long-running inference services

Limitations & Open Questions

This is an early-stage exploration. Challenges include overhead, false positives, and defining what “faulty” truly means in stochastic systems.
